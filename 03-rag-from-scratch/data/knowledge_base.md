# AgriRobo Farm Planning Knowledge Base

## Tower System Specifications
Each tower has 6 modules and 18 net cups. Reservoir holds 26 litres per tower.
Pump inside pushes water up, gravity returns it. Nutrients used: Katyayani raw salt kit.
Location: Gurgaon, Delhi NCR. Proven crops: lettuce, cauliflower, coriander, karela.

## Daily Consumption for Family of 6

Tomato: 400-500g daily, 3-3.5kg per week. Used in every dal and sabzi base. Highest consumption vegetable.
Coriander: 20-30g daily, 150-200g per week. Garnish on every dish, used every single day.
Green chilli: 20-30g daily, 150g per week. Every dish, every day.
Spinach: 300-400g per use, 3 times per week, about 1kg per week total.
Methi: 200-300g per use, 2 times per week, about 500g per week total.
Mint: 20-30g daily, 150g per week. Chutney, raita, chai.
Cucumber: 150-200g per use, 4 times per week, about 700g per week.
Karela: 200-300g per use, 2 times per week, about 500g per week.
Capsicum: 150-200g per use, 2 times per week, about 400g per week.
Lettuce: 100-150g per use, 3 times per week, about 400g per week.

## Gurgaon Seasonal Calendar

October to March: Best growing window. Temperature 8-28 degrees Celsius.
All leafy greens thrive: spinach, coriander, methi, lettuce, palak.
Tomato and capsicum peak in this season.

April to June: Critical problem season. Temperature 38-46 degrees Celsius.
Leafy greens cannot survive outdoors. They bolt within days.
Spinach, coriander, methi, lettuce cannot grow in this period.
Green chilli and brinjal thrive in heat. Karela and cucumber peak.

July to September: Monsoon season. Temperature 28-38 degrees Celsius, high humidity.
Leafy greens restart carefully. Fungal risk is high.
Karela, toori, cucumber thrive in July and August.
Change reservoir every 10 days, increase airflow to prevent fungal issues.

## Crop Type Classification

Type A - Full Harvest Leafy: Spinach, Methi, Lettuce, Palak, Beans.
Plant entire tower, grow, harvest entire tower at once, then replant.
Must stagger multiple towers so harvests spread over time.

Type B - Cut and Come Again Herb: Coriander, Mint, Tulsi, Basil.
Plant once, snip regularly, inner plant regrows. Never fully harvested.
One or two towers lasts a family for months.

Type C - Continuous Fruiting: Tomato, Karela, Cucumber, Capsicum, Green chilli, Brinjal.
Plant takes time to establish, fruits for weeks, then plant exhausts and must be replaced.
Stagger so replacement plants are always in pipeline.

## Tower Count Calculations

Tomato: Needs 3000-3500g per week. Each plant yields 400g per week when fruiting.
Requires 8-9 plants fruiting simultaneously. Two plants per tower.
Needs 4-5 towers actively fruiting plus 1-2 towers always in growth pipeline.
Total: 5-6 tomato towers. 75-90 day gap from transplant to first fruit.

Spinach: Needs 1000g per week. Tower yields 1200g at harvest. 30-day cycle.
Needs 1 harvest every 8-9 days. Cycle is 4.3 weeks.
Total: 4 spinach towers in rotation.

Coriander: Needs 150-200g per week. Managed as cut-and-come-again.
Weekly snip yield per tower is 100-150g.
Total: 2 coriander towers sufficient.

Methi: Needs 500g per week. Tower yields 900g at harvest. 30-day cycle.
Total: 2 methi towers in rotation.

## EC and pH Guidelines

EC stands for Electrical Conductivity, measured in millisiemens per centimeter.
Leafy greens prefer low EC: 1.0 to 2.0 mS/cm.
Fruiting vegetables prefer higher EC: 2.0 to 3.5 mS/cm.
Herbs prefer medium EC: 1.5 to 2.5 mS/cm.

pH optimal range for most crops: 5.5 to 6.5.
Below 5.5 causes nutrient lockout. Above 6.8 causes iron and manganese deficiency.
Check pH and EC every 2 days minimum. Adjust nutrients accordingly.

## Karela Growing Protocol

Karela confirmed working in AgriRobo tower. Use 2 vines per tower maximum.
Karela is a seasonal crop. Best in April to August.
Needs trellis support. Grows vigorously once established.
Yields approximately 500g per tower per week once fruiting.
Peak season yield can reach 800g per tower per week.
